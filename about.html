About Kgokagano-Sechaba

Together we support, empower, and protect survivors of Gender-Based Violence.

Who We Are

Kgokagano-Sechaba is a non-profit organization addressing Gender-Based Violence (GBV) specifically in the Lawley Extension 2 community and surrounding areas. Our mission is to empower individuals, particularly women and survivors, to break free from abuse and build a brighter future.

Key Components

Problem Statement

GBV is a serious issue in South Africa, with many individuals facing physical, emotional, and psychological abuse.

Goals & Objectives

Short-term: Provide shelter, skills programs, and counseling. Long-term: Reduce GBV, empower survivors, and build a culture of respect.

Programs & Services

Shelter services, skills development, entrepreneurship, counseling, and emotional support.

Target Market

Women and survivors of Gender-Based Violence within the community.

Funding Strategy

Grants, donations, fundraising events, and partnerships.

Marketing Strategy

Social media, community outreach, and partnerships with local organizations.

Code of Conduct

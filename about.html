About Kgokagano-Sechaba

Together we support, empower and protect survivors of gender-based violence in Lawley Extension 2 and the surrounding communities.

Who We Are

Kgokagano-Sechaba Community Centre is a registered non-profit organisation working to prevent and respond to gender-based violence in Lawley Extension 2 and neighbouring areas. We deliver trauma-informed counselling, safe temporary shelter, legal referrals and skills development programs to help survivors rebuild safe, independent lives.

Key Components

Problem Statement

Gender-based violence remains a serious and pervasive issue across South Africa. Many survivors face barriers to safety, justice and economic opportunity.

Goals & Objectives

Short-term: provide immediate safety, counselling and economic support. Long-term: reduce GBV in the community through prevention programs, survivor empowerment and partnerships.

Programs & Services

Temporary shelter, trauma-informed counselling, life and vocational skills training, legal advocacy and community awareness efforts.

Target Group

Women and survivors of gender-based violence, particularly those living in or near Lawley Extension 2 who need confidential support and pathways to recovery.

Funding Strategy

We fund our work through grants, donations, fundraising events and strategic partnerships with local organisations.

Outreach & Partnerships

We reach communities through social media, local events, schools and collaboration with service providers and civic groups.

Code of Conduct

Confidentiality: We protect sensitive information and only share when required for safety or by law.

Professionalism: All staff and volunteers act with integrity and respect.

Non-Discrimination: We welcome and support all survivors without bias.

Transparency: We maintain clear reporting and financial accountability.

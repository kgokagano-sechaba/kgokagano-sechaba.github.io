Breaking the Cycle of Gender-Based Violence

Kgokagano-Sechaba supports survivors across South Africa — providing shelter, counselling, legal referrals and skills development for long-term recovery.

How We Help

Counselling Services

Confidential emotional and psychological support — trauma-informed therapy and peer support groups to help survivors regain stability.

Skills Development

Life skills, vocational training, and entrepreneurship programs that empower survivors toward economic independence.

Safe Shelter

Temporary safe housing, food and case management for survivors escaping immediate danger — with coordinated referrals to police and social services.

Awareness Programs

The Reality of GBV in South Africa

1 in 3

South African women will experience gender-based violence in their lifetime.

Every 3 Hours

A woman is murdered in South Africa — frequently by a partner or someone known to her.

50%+

Many sexual assault cases involve youth; survivors under 21 are disproportionately affected.

Under 8%

A small proportion of GBV reports lead to conviction; survivors need accessible legal support and advocacy.

Take Action

If you or someone you know is experiencing abuse, please reach out immediately. Confidential reporting and support are available.

GBV in South Africa

South Africa faces high rates of gender-based violence. Survivors encounter challenges including limited access to legal support, poverty and unsafe environments. Kgokagano-Sechaba provides trauma-informed counseling, temporary shelter, legal advocacy and community awareness programs.

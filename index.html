Breaking the Cycle of Gender-Based Violence

Kgokagano-Sechaba supports survivors of GBV across South Africa — providing shelter, counselling, legal referrals, and skills development.

How We Help

Counselling Services

Confidential emotional and psychological support to help survivors regain safety and mental wellbeing. Trauma-informed therapists and peer-support groups.

Learn More

Skills Development

Life skills, vocational training, and entrepreneurship programs that empower survivors to achieve economic independence.

Learn More

Safe Shelter

Temporary safe housing, food, and case management for survivors escaping immediate danger — with coordinated police and social services referrals.

Learn More

Awareness Programs

Community workshops, school programs, and local campaigns aimed at prevention, bystander intervention, and community accountability.

The Reality of GBV in South Africa

1 in 3

South African women will experience gender-based violence in their lifetime.

Every 3 Hours

A woman is murdered in South Africa — frequently by a partner or someone known to her.

50%+

Many sexual assault cases involve youth; survivors under 21 are disproportionately affected.

Under 8%

A small proportion of GBV reports lead to conviction; survivors need accessible legal support and advocacy.

Take Action

If you or someone you know is experiencing abuse, please reach out. Immediate assistance and confidential reporting are available.

GBV in South Africa

South Africa faces high rates of gender-based violence. Survivors often encounter challenges such as limited access to legal support, poverty, and unsafe environments. Kgokagano-Sechaba provides trauma-informed counseling, temporary shelter, legal advocacy, and community awareness programs.
